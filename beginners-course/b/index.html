The Six Frameworks That Make Independence Possible

This is the part that separates Ian's teaching from everything else in the market. These are not vague concepts. They are named, specific, repeatable systems:

1. The Layers of Looking

How to build any sketch in five to seven stages - from blank page to finished piece. Each layer has a defined job: skeleton, information, depth, atmosphere, animation. You do not skip layers. You do not get lost. You follow the process, and the sketch builds itself.

2. The Four Pillars

The structural foundation every sketch stands on: Perspective, Composition, Detail, Tonal Value. Perspective and Composition go in first. Detail and Tone are developed by bouncing between them. Once you see this hierarchy, you can diagnose any scene.

3. The Editor's Eye

Why what you leave out matters more than what you put in. Ian teaches a five-filter diagnostic: Is it too vibrant? Too abstract? Too dominant? Too ambiguous? Uninteresting? If yes - leave it out. This is where beginners become artists.

4. The Colour Family

How three colours create infinite harmony - no colour-matching anxiety, no clashing, guaranteed. You will build four completely different palettes across four workshops and create your own colour charts.

5. The Boldness Paradox

The moment your hand freezes - the colour feels too strong, the line feels too thick, every instinct screams "pull back" - that is the moment to push forward. Wobbly lines become charm. "Wrong" colours transform through layering. Bold marks create drama.

6. The Storytelling Sketch

You are not drawing buildings. You are telling the story of a place. Ian teaches five story components: historical elements, modern elements, functional elements, cultural elements, atmospheric elements. A lamp post. A cobblestone. A sign above a door. These are not details - they are narrative.
