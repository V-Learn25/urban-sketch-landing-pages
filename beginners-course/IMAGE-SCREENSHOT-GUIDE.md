# Beginners Course Landing Page - Screenshot & Image Guide

> Use this guide to scrub through the course videos and capture high-resolution screenshots.
> Each moment includes the video number, what to look for on screen, a transcript quote to help locate it, and a suggested caption for the landing page.

---

## How to Use This Guide

1. Open the relevant video in the course platform
2. Search for the quoted transcript text (or just scrub to the described stage)
3. Capture a high-res screenshot showing Ian in the PiP corner + the sketch in progress
4. Name files using the convention: `workshop-stage.jpg` (e.g., `english-bridge-first-wash.jpg`)

## WORKSHOP 1: ENGLISH BRIDGE (12 videos)

### EB-1: First Pencil Strokes on Blank Page
- **Video:** Studio Step 1 - Pencil Outline (early)
- **Find this moment:** "Very, very light, very careful, see? Like that, and that's the top of the bridge coming down"
- **What you'll see:** Nearly blank A3 page with just two converging pencil lines (top and bottom of bridge) and first arches being placed
- **Why it works:** The "I can do that" moment. Shows even Ian starts with two wobbly lines.
- **Caption:** "Every sketch starts here. Two simple pencil lines."
- **Priority:** HIGH - use as the "before" in before/after pairs

### EB-2: Completed Pencil Outline
- **Video:** End of Studio Step 1
- **Find this moment:** "and that everybody is all we need to do for step number one. So let me just recap a few things. We started off by putting in the structure of the bridge"
- **What you'll see:** Complete pencil outline - bridge arches getting smaller (perspective), church spire above, wobbly reflection lines in water. No ink, no colour.
- **Why it works:** Shows the Four Pillars at work (perspective, composition, detail, tone). Still approachable, yet already recognisably a bridge scene.
- **Caption:** "Step 1 complete. Pencil only. Perspective, composition, and structure - all in place."

### EB-3: First Pen Pass Complete (0.3 Fineliner)
- **Video:** End of Studio Step 2
- **Find this moment:** "just to go over the pencil sketch with a permanent fine liner, and adding more information. There's a lot more information in the sketch now"
- **What you'll see:** Pencil outline reinforced with permanent ink. Brickwork detail appearing, fish sculptures between arches, railings, wobbly ink reflections in water. Still no colour.
- **Why it works:** Most dramatic before/after within the drawing stages. Sketch jumps from tentative pencil to bold, characterful ink.
- **Caption:** "One pen. Permanent ink over pencil. The sketch comes alive."

### EB-4: First Watercolour Wash (THE MONEY SHOT)
- **Video:** Watercolour Stage (Step 4) - first washes
- **Find this moment:** "I'm going to start off by just doing a little bit of Prussian blue... just pushing it around, just teasing it around the picture... I'm going to go in now with a mixture of cobalt turquoise light and orange"
- **What you'll see:** Prussian blue washes across sky and bridge top. Orange and cobalt turquoise bleeding into each other in the water. Wet and alive. Black ink lines still crisp underneath.
- **Why it works:** Single most visually dramatic moment. Monochrome to colour. Only 3 colours being used - demonstrates the Colour Family framework live.
- **Caption:** "Just three colours. Watch the sketch transform."
- **Priority:** HIGH - best scroll-stopper for the page

### EB-5: Dark Arches Painted (Tonal Depth)
- **Video:** Watercolour Stage - painting dark arches
- **Find this moment:** "I want this to be really dark... Prussian blue mixed with the orange, but I'm mixing it in a different way. I'm putting a lot more paint on, less water... Check this out. Check this out. Wow."
- **What you'll see:** Rich, dark mixture under the arches. Dramatic contrast between pale bridge surface and dark shadows. Reflections echoing the darkness in the water.
- **Why it works:** The Boldness Paradox in action. Ian's own excitement ("Check this out. Wow.") tells you this is the payoff.
- **Caption:** "Bold darks create depth. Same three colours, mixed thicker."

### EB-6: Finished Sketch
- **Video:** End of watercolour stage
- **Find this moment:** "just using those three colours, just the cobalt turquoise light, the Prussian blue and the transparent orange... Hopefully it's given me quite a rich variety of tones"
- **What you'll see:** Complete English Bridge - warm orange on church and stonework, cool blue in sky and shadows, green (orange + turquoise) in water reflections, dark dramatic arches, church spire reaching up.
- **Why it works:** Hero shot. Combined with EB-1 creates the ultimate before/after.
- **Caption:** "The finished sketch. Three colours. One pen. One afternoon. You can do this."
- **Priority:** HIGH - hero image

## WORKSHOP 2: TANNERS WINE MERCHANTS (12 videos)

### TW-1: The Pencil Framework
- **Video:** Studio Step 1 - Pencil
- **Find this moment:** "So this pencil line here is the left-hand edge of that building as it wraps around. And then the hill goes up like that. And then the top of the buildings are like that. So that is the basic structure, the framework"
- **What you'll see:** Near-blank A3 page with just a few light pencil lines dividing the space into zones. Vertical building corner, sweep of the hill, roofline.
- **Caption:** "Three pencil lines. That's how this Tudor street begins."
- **Priority:** HIGH - the "before"

### TW-2: Tudor Beams in Pen
- **Video:** Step 2 - First pen pass
- **Find this moment:** "So do you like these little semi-circles that bounce around? See like that?... How cute is that?"
- **What you'll see:** Bold pen lines over pencil. Distinctive Tudor half-timber beams with curved decorative elements. "Tanners Wine Merchants" lettering in chunky outline. Black and white, no colour, but already beautiful.
- **Caption:** "Watch the building come alive as Ian adds the iconic Tudor beams."

### TW-3: The Editor's Eye in Action
- **Video:** Referenced in both location and studio sections
- **Find this moment:** "if you look underneath that, they're advertising their Instagram account with all these like fluorescent colours. That's not going to go in the picture because that is a distraction"
- **What you'll see:** The pen work is detailed on the Tudor beams above, but the Instagram ads area below is deliberately left blank/simplified.
- **Why it works:** The Editor's Eye framework made visible. Shows beginners there's a METHOD to choosing what goes in a sketch.
- **Caption:** "The secret beginners miss: knowing what to leave OUT."

### TW-4: Watercolour Explosion - Bold Blue (SCROLL-STOPPER)
- **Video:** Watercolour stage
- **Find this moment:** "Look at this. Now we're talking. That's just the blue... really strong blue. In fact, I'm going to make it even stronger. So if you do something like this, you think, oh my goodness, that's so strong. Make it stronger because that's where the fun comes."
- **What you'll see:** Dramatic Ultramarine Blue washes on left building. Magnesium Brown on Tanners building. The transformation from monochrome to vivid colour is striking.
- **Why it works:** Most dramatic visual transformation in this workshop. The "too strong" blue is eye-catching. Ian's "make it stronger" is counterintuitive advice that hooks people.
- **Caption:** "Just 3 colours. Ian shows you why 'too bold' is exactly right."
- **Priority:** HIGH - best scroll-stopper for this workshop

### TW-5: Pen Over Paint (Layered Multimedia)
- **Video:** Final pen stage - drawing on top of dried paint
- **Find this moment:** "draw on top of the paint... I'm going to really start thickening up now some of these beams... it's very much kind of like a layered multimedia approach"
- **What you'll see:** Bold pen lines sitting on top of watercolour washes. Tudor beams thick and emphatic over blue and brown paint. Hatching adding texture to roofs and pavements.
- **Why it works:** A technique most beginners would never think of - drawing ON TOP of paint.
- **Caption:** "The technique that makes Urban Sketching unique: drawing ON TOP of the paint."

### TW-6: Finished Sketch
- **Video:** Final recap
- **Find this moment:** "And here is the completed sketch from our second workshop... Then we put the colour on using three very specific colours, the Magnesium Brown, the Ultramarine Blue and the Alizarin Crimson"
- **What you'll see:** Rich Tudor half-timber architecture. Bold blue, warm browns, crimson tones. Detailed beams, semi-circles, signage, textured pavements, chimney pots, hanging baskets. Full layered multimedia look.
- **Caption:** "From a blank page to this - using just 3 colours and Ian's step-by-step process."
- **Priority:** HIGH - hero image

## WORKSHOP 3: CHURCH STREET (14 videos)

### CS-1: Perspective Framework
- **Video:** Video 4 of 14 - "Pencil"
- **Find this moment:** "You've got your top of your building, bottom of the building, edge of the curb on this side... you've got your vanishing point here and your vanishing point over there"
- **What you'll see:** Light pencil lines only - the long street receding into the distance. Perspective lines clearly visible. Page mostly empty.
- **Why it works:** Shows the "invisible architecture" that makes depth believable. Demystifies perspective.
- **Caption:** "Every stunning street scene starts with just 3 pencil lines and a vanishing point."
- **Priority:** HIGH - the "before"

### CS-2: First Pen Pass Complete
- **Video:** Video 5 of 14 - End of "Pen 1"
- **Find this moment:** "Okay guys and that is step number two. Using the 0.3 fine liner pen."
- **What you'll see:** Fine liner over pencil - timber frames, church spire, hanging baskets, bollard, cobbles. Line work only, no colour. The long perspective down Church Street is clearly visible with receding rooftops.
- **Caption:** "One pen. One pass. The street already pulls you in."

### CS-3: First Watercolour Washes
- **Video:** Video 9 of 14 - "Watercolour"
- **Find this moment:** "I'm painting inside the shapes and I'm kind of going over them as well... just really letting the paints come out to play"
- **What you'll see:** Translucent washes of Winsor Blue, Hooker's Green, and Burnt Sienna on the page. Timber structures show blue. Church end has brown washes. Greens softening parts. Sketch is wet and alive - colour pooling and mixing.
- **Why it works:** Most dramatic transformation - line work to atmospheric colour. Limited palette approach (3 colours) is the selling point.
- **Caption:** "Just 3 watercolours transform pen lines into atmosphere - and it only took minutes."
- **Priority:** HIGH

### CS-4: Brush Pens Creating Depth (NEW TOOL UNLOCKED)
- **Video:** Video 11 of 14 - "Brush Pens"
- **Find this moment:** "Hopefully you can imagine walking into the scene a little bit more now, because it has a lot more tonal variety"
- **What you'll see:** Dry watercolour overlaid with Tombow brush pen greys. Windows pop, roofs have weight, street recedes with atmospheric depth. Dramatically more "real" than the flat watercolour stage.
- **Why it works:** Brush pens introduced for the FIRST TIME in the course. The tonal variety transforms a pleasant watercolour into something three-dimensional.
- **Caption:** "The secret to making your sketches feel 3D? Grey brush pens over watercolour. Game-changer."
- **Priority:** HIGH - technique spotlight

### CS-5: Grey Hatching Expansion
- **Video:** Video 11 of 14 - "Brush Pens" (late)
- **Find this moment:** "I'm going to use the N79 Tombow brush pen. And I'm just going to put a very, very light grey hatch over this... it's kind of expanded, it's expanded the picture"
- **What you'll see:** The Wildlife Rescue building (previously left white) now has a light grey wash/hatch. The sketch has "expanded" outward.
- **Caption:** "One light grey hatch and the whole sketch opens up."

### CS-6: Finished Sketch
- **Video:** Video 14 of 14 - "Finished Work"
- **Find this moment:** "And here we have the finished sketch of Church Street in Shrewsbury, which has been great fun to do"
- **What you'll see:** Fully complete - rich textures: cobbles, timber frames, brickwork, hanging baskets, church spire, pub signs, lettering. The long perspective pulls the eye from foreground cobbles right through to the church at the far end.
- **Caption:** "From a blank page to this - built step by step, using techniques any beginner can learn."
- **Priority:** HIGH - hero image

## WORKSHOP 4: WYLE COP (13 videos)

### WC-1: Pencil Structure - The Steep Hill
- **Video:** Studio Step 1 - Pencil
- **Find this moment:** "Very lightly, very carefully, let's just bring this line down here. That breaks up the space already. Pavement is going to go about here which is roughly about a third of the way up the page"
- **What you'll see:** Sweeping perspective lines showing the dramatic hill climbing upward. Building edges and pavement curves. Mostly white page with a few confident pencil strokes.
- **Caption:** "A few pencil lines and the steep hill of Wyle Cop already comes alive."
- **Priority:** HIGH - the "before"

### WC-2: Bold First Colour Wash (BOLDNESS PARADOX)
- **Video:** Watercolour stage - initial washes
- **Find this moment:** "Oh, look at this, Transparent Orange. Now, I know the building in front of me on Wild Cop is not Transparent Orange, so this is a huge exaggeration... Wow, look at that. Gorgeous."
- **What you'll see:** Vivid washes of transparent orange and cerulean blue over the buildings. Colours are striking and "wrong" compared to reality. Looks dramatic and almost reckless. Colours bleeding into each other.
- **Why it works:** The Boldness Paradox in action. Colours look "wrong" but breathtaking. Demonstrates the Colour Family framework.
- **Caption:** "Three colours. That's all. Cerulean blue, transparent orange, and Windsor violet - layered boldly."
- **Priority:** HIGH - scroll-stopper

### WC-3: Brush Pens Over Colour (Depth Stage)
- **Video:** Brush pen stage
- **Find this moment:** "The watercolor paint has dried... So now we're going to work on top of the paints with the Tombow brush pens... start adding a bit more depth and more of a three-dimensional aspect"
- **What you'll see:** Vibrant watercolour with mid-grey and dark brush pen tones layered on top. Shop windows dark, buildings have 3D weight, church tower standing out.
- **Caption:** "Watch the moment colour becomes architecture. Brush pens bring the buildings to life."

### WC-4: Church Tower Through Hatching
- **Video:** Detail pen stage
- **Find this moment:** "I'll just use a little hatching technique there, just to add a bit more tone to this particular part, so it stands out more... So that really emphasises the church, so much more now"
- **What you'll see:** Church tower clearly defined above roofline with hatching creating tonal contrast. Trees suggested with curly marks. The focal point is fully realised.
- **Caption:** "The church tower Ian spotted on location now commands the skyline."

### WC-5: Finished Sketch (THE GRAND FINALE)
- **Video:** Final reveal
- **Find this moment:** "And this is my finished sketch of Wild Cop in Shrewsbury, breaking it down into lots and lots of steps"
- **What you'll see:** Complete steep hill scene - cobblestones in foreground, buildings climbing up with shop fronts, windows, signage ("Whiskey"), hanging baskets, sandwich board, chunky pavements, church tower presiding over everything.
- **Why it works:** This is Workshop 4 - the culmination of the entire course. Everything the student has learned comes together here.
- **Caption:** "From a blank page to this. Every line, every wash, every detail - built step by step."
- **Priority:** HIGH - hero image / course finale

### WC-6: Colour Family Exercise (3 Colours + Mixes)
- **Video:** Colour mixing exercise (before watercolour stage)
- **Find this moment:** "There's the cerulean blue. There is the transparent orange... And there is the Windsor violets... Wow, look at that. Isn't that beautiful?"
- **What you'll see:** NOT the main sketch - a separate exercise showing three pure colours plus all their mixes on white paper. Patches of blue, orange, violet, plus chocolatey brown, purpley brown, and muted three-colour mix.
- **Why it works:** The Colour Family framework made visual in one image. Answers "how do you get all those colours from just 3 paints?"
- **Caption:** "Just three colours. Mix them together and you unlock an entire palette."
- **Priority:** MEDIUM - great for the Six Frameworks section
