Why This Course Is Different

This Urban Sketching for Beginners course has some notable differences to any other courses you may have taken:

A Focus on Fundamentals

Ian assumes no prior knowledge or skills. You'll begin in the most simplistic form, with a pencil and a blank page. Then, Ian guides you from the very basics — showing you how to loosely sketch out your lines and shapes, before slowly and methodically building up your picture, piece-by-piece.

Pencil Sketching

For the first time, Ian starts each workshop with a pencil and rubber, instead of a fine liner. This means you get to make mistakes, rub out and redo until you have your basic sketch down. If trying to be too perfect has held you back, these first lessons will be a game-changer.

Skill Building Exercises

Each exercise starts with a demonstration from Ian, then deep dives into a specific technique that you will practise separately, before applying it to your main picture. Exercises include hatching, drawing perspective, penwork, drawing on paint, understanding colour, and more.

Progressive Learning

Each carefully crafted lesson builds on the previous one through four comprehensive workshops. By the time you complete the final workshop, you will have developed the skills and confidence to sketch with a high degree of competence.
