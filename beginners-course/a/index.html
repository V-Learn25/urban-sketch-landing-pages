The 6 Named Frameworks That Make This System Transferable

This is the part most art courses completely miss. They show you how to draw one thing. Then you are on your own. Ian does the opposite. He teaches you 6 specific, named frameworks you can apply to any scene, anywhere in the world, for the rest of your life:

1. The Layers of Looking

How to build any sketch in five to seven stages from blank page to finished piece. Each layer has a defined job. No guesswork. No staring at a blank page wondering where to start.

2. The Four Pillars

The 4 structural laws behind every sketch that makes people say "how did you DO that?" Master these and you will walk down a street you have walked a thousand times and suddenly see a sketch you cannot wait to draw.

3. The Editor's Eye

What to leave out matters more than what you put in. This five-filter system trains you to see like an artist, not a photocopier.

4. The Colour Family

Three colours. That is it. Three colours create infinite harmony. No more standing in the art shop overwhelmed by 87 shades of blue.

5. The Boldness Paradox

Ian's counterintuitive rule for the exact moment when 90% of beginners ruin a good sketch by playing it safe. It is not about adding more detail. It is about doing the one thing your instincts scream at you NOT to do.

6. The Storytelling Sketch

Why the sketch that "breaks all the rules" often has more soul than the technically perfect one. And the specific 3-question test Ian uses to decide what story any scene is trying to tell you.

Here is why these frameworks matter so much: by Workshop 4, you do not need Ian telling you what to do. You have the system. You can walk up to any scene - a cafe in Paris, a harbour in Maine, your own back garden - and you know exactly how to start, how to build, and how to finish.
